The PvZ mid game is defined by Protoss seizing air control with corsairs and using that advantage to launch devastating harassment while building toward the critical storm tech. This is the phase where Protoss must be active and aggressive -- passive play allows Zerg to drone freely, take additional bases, and reach the Hive tech that shifts the matchup in Zerg's favor. Every second of the mid game matters, and the corsair is the unit that makes Protoss's entire strategy possible.

## Corsair Play

Corsairs are the backbone of PvZ from the moment they leave the Stargate until the final engagement of the game. Their primary role is killing overlords. Overlords provide Zerg with both supply and map vision, so destroying them accomplishes two things simultaneously: it supply-blocks Zerg, preventing production of new units, and it removes the scouting network that lets Zerg see Protoss army movements. A Protoss player who produces six to eight corsairs and actively hunts overlords across the map creates enormous strategic pressure.

Corsairs also counter mutalisks directly. If Zerg invested in mutalisk tech, corsairs shut it down -- corsairs deal splash damage to air units, meaning they become more efficient the more mutalisks are clumped together. Disruption Web, the corsair's special ability, disables ground-based anti-air like Spore Colonies, allowing corsairs to fly over any base freely. This ability becomes critical when combined with Dark Templar or storm drops that need a clear path.

## The Bisu Build (Corsair into DT)

Named after the legendary Protoss player Bisu, this build revolutionized PvZ when it was introduced. The sequence is straightforward: corsairs kill overlords across the map, removing Zerg's mobile detection, and then Dark Templar are sent into Zerg's bases. Without overlords nearby, Zerg has no way to see the DTs, and a single Dark Templar in a mineral line will destroy the entire drone workforce in seconds.

Even if Zerg anticipates the DTs and builds Spore Colonies at every base, the strategy still succeeds in its broader goal. Spore Colonies cost resources and drone time that could have gone into army or economy, and they are fixed defenses that DTs can simply walk around to attack undefended positions. The transition after DTs is to merge them into Archons for the ground army while adding High Templar for Psionic Storm. The Bisu build gave Protoss a reliable mid-game plan in a matchup that was previously considered Zerg-favored.

## Storm Drops

Shuttle plus High Templar drops into Zerg mineral lines are one of the most efficient forms of harassment in Brood War. A single Psionic Storm on a cluster of drones kills most of them instantly, dealing economic damage that takes Zerg dozens of seconds to recover from. Multiple storms across different bases can cripple Zerg's economy beyond recovery.

Effective storm drops require corsair air control. Without corsairs clearing the sky, Scourge or mutalisks will kill the Shuttle before it reaches the target. The standard execution is to send corsairs ahead to kill any Scourge waiting along the flight path, then follow with the Shuttle. Two High Templar in a single Shuttle provides enough energy for four storms -- enough to devastate two mineral lines in one trip.

## Expansion Timing

Protoss's third base should go down once corsair numbers are established and the initial harassment has begun. The mid game is expensive -- corsair production, tech buildings, and storm research all require significant investment -- so a third base is necessary to sustain it. The fourth base needs to be secured before Zerg reaches Hive tech, because once Defilers arrive with Dark Swarm, Protoss's ground army becomes significantly less effective and the game dynamic shifts.

## Dealing with Mass Hydralisk

Hydralisks are Zerg's primary ground army in PvZ. They are fast, deal strong ranged damage, and are produced in large numbers from multiple hatcheries. The counter is Psionic Storm. A single storm on a clumped hydralisk group deals devastating damage because hydralisks have relatively low health. The standard engagement is zealots with speed upgrade tanking in front while High Templar cast storms behind them. Reavers can supplement this composition, using Scarabs to zone hydralisks and punish clumping.

## Common Mistakes

Not producing corsairs concedes air control to mutalisks and leaves Zerg's overlord network intact, giving them full map vision while Protoss operates blind. Late storm tech is equally punishing -- without storms, hydralisk armies roll over zealot-based ground forces. Friendly fire with storms, hitting your own zealots engaged in melee with hydralisks, wastes energy and kills your own army. Storm placement requires precision and practice.
