## Overview

PvZ is a matchup that pits Protoss's superior technology and powerful individual units against Zerg's overwhelming numbers and economic efficiency. Protoss must leverage tech advantages — corsairs, reavers, dark templar, and high templar — to control the game before Zerg's economy spirals out of control. Zerg aims to survive early Protoss aggression, establish a massive economic lead with multiple hatcheries, and overwhelm Protoss with waves of hydralisks, lurkers, and eventually defilers. The matchup was revolutionized by Bisu's corsair-dark templar build, which fundamentally changed how Protoss approaches the early game.

## Key Concepts

### Protoss Goals
- **Tech pressure**: Use corsairs, reavers, and dark templar to harass and deny Zerg's economy
- **Storm drops**: High Templar dropped into Zerg bases devastate clumped hydralisks and drones
- **Third base timing**: Protoss must take a third base to keep up with Zerg's economy, but timing it correctly is critical
- **Archon/Zealot army**: The core late-game composition that trades efficiently against Zerg ground armies

### Zerg Goals
- **Economic dominance**: Take as many bases as possible and produce from multiple hatcheries
- **Hydralisk-based defense**: Hydralisks are the backbone of ZvP, effective against both ground and air Protoss units
- **Lurker containment**: Lurkers at key positions can shut down Protoss ground movement
- **Defiler tech**: Dark Swarm and Plague in the late game neutralize Protoss's ranged firepower

## Common Openings

### Protoss
- **Forge Fast Expand (FFE)**: The standard PvZ opening. Forge first into a fast nexus at the natural, with photon cannons providing defense. This gives Protoss a safe economic start and smooth tech transitions.
- **2 Gate Zealot Pressure**: Two gateways producing zealots for early pressure. Aims to delay Zerg's third base or force early sunkens, buying time for Protoss tech.
- **Bisu Build (Corsair/DT)**: The revolutionary build pioneered by Bisu. Fast stargate into corsairs for overlord hunting, combined with dark templar harass. Devastates Zerg if detection is late, and the corsairs provide lasting air control.

### Zerg
- **3 Hatch Hydra**: Three hatcheries before pool, transitioning into fast hydralisk production. The standard economic response that gives Zerg enough production to handle most Protoss builds.
- **5 Hatch Hydra**: An extremely greedy five-hatchery build that floods the map with hydralisks. Risky against early aggression but creates an overwhelming army if left unchecked.
- **2 Hatch Lurker**: Faster lurker tech from two bases. A more defensive approach that prioritizes holding the natural expansion and denying Protoss scouting.

## Mid-Game Transitions

The mid-game is defined by Protoss's tech choices and Zerg's response:

- **Corsair/Reaver**: Protoss uses corsairs to deny overlord scouting and control the air while reavers in shuttles harass Zerg's mineral lines. This forces Zerg to build hydralisks and scourge, pulling resources from drone production.
- **Storm drops**: Once Psionic Storm is researched, high templar dropped into Zerg's main or natural can destroy entire hydralisk armies. Zerg must spread units and maintain overlord coverage to spot incoming drops.
- **Protoss third base timing**: Protoss needs a third base to sustain production but taking it too early invites Zerg aggression. The standard timing depends on the opening build and how much damage Protoss's harassment inflicted.

Zerg responds by:
- **Scourge production**: Scourge counter corsairs and can snipe shuttles carrying reavers or high templar
- **Hydra/lurker positioning**: Spreading lurkers at key paths and choke points to slow Protoss army movement
- **Counter-attacks**: Zerg's superior production allows for attacking multiple locations simultaneously

## Late Game

The PvZ late game is a dramatic battle of attrition:

- **High Templar storms**: Psionic Storm remains Protoss's most devastating ability against clumped Zerg units. A well-placed storm on hydralisks or zerglings can turn a battle instantly.
- **Archon/Zealot composition**: Archons with their splash damage and zealots with speed upgrade form the core Protoss army. This composition trades extremely well against hydralisk/zergling swarms.
- **Defiler entrance**: Once Zerg reaches hive and defiler tech, Dark Swarm negates all ranged attacks, making hydralisks and zerglings effectively invincible under the cloud. Protoss must use storms and archon splash to fight under swarm.
- **Expansion control**: The player who controls more bases wins the long game. Zerg's ability to remax faster gives them an advantage in attrition wars, but Protoss's superior army quality means each engagement matters.

## Key Players

- **Bisu**: The revolutionary PvZ player who invented the corsair/DT opening. His 2007 MSL victory over Savior changed the matchup forever and is considered one of the greatest series in Brood War history.
- **Rain**: Modern PvZ excellence. Known for impeccable macro, precise storm drops, and consistent performance against top Zerg players in ASL.
- **Soulkey**: Elite ZvP player whose hydralisk micro, defiler control, and economic management represent the peak of Zerg play in the matchup.
