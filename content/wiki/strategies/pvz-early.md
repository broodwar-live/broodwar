The early game in PvZ establishes the economic and strategic foundation for the entire match. Protoss must balance the need for a fast expansion against the threat of early Zerg aggression. The matchup's defining tension begins immediately: Protoss units are individually powerful but expensive and slow to produce, while Zerg can flood the map with cheap zerglings from multiple hatcheries. Getting the opening wrong -- too greedy without defense, or too defensive without economy -- puts Protoss at a disadvantage that compounds through the mid game.

## Opening Builds

### FFE (Forge Fast Expand)

The standard and most common opening in PvZ. Protoss builds a Forge first, places one or two Photon Cannons at the natural expansion choke, and drops a Nexus behind that defense. The Forge wall and cannons hold off any early zergling pressure while the second base's economy kicks in. After the Nexus, a Gateway goes down followed by the Cybernetics Core to begin teching. FFE sacrifices early aggression entirely in exchange for a safe economic start. The build is reliable against nearly everything Zerg can do, which is why it has been the dominant PvZ opening in professional play for over a decade.

### 2 Gate Zealot Pressure

An aggressive alternative to FFE. Two Gateways produce zealots that move across the map to pressure Zerg's natural or deny the third hatchery. On maps with short rush distances, this build can deal significant drone damage or force Zerg to build sunkens instead of drones. The goal is not to kill Zerg outright but to delay their economy long enough for Protoss to transition into tech with a lead. The risk is that if the zealots accomplish nothing -- Zerg builds a sunken and loses no drones -- Protoss is behind because they delayed their expansion and tech.

### 1 Gate Core

Gateway into Cybernetics Core opens a flexible tech path. From the Core, Protoss can choose Robotics Facility for reavers, Stargate for corsairs, or Citadel of Adun for zealot speed. This opening is less common than FFE but offers earlier tech timing at the cost of a delayed expansion. It is most effective when Protoss has specific information about Zerg's build from scouting and wants to exploit it.

## Scouting

Probe scouting reads Zerg's opening and determines how Protoss should respond. The key information is pool timing, gas timing, and hatchery count. Three hatcheries before a Spawning Pool is extremely greedy from Zerg -- zealot pressure can punish this severely. Early gas extraction signals mutalisk tech, meaning Protoss must plan corsairs or defensive cannons at mineral lines. A standard 12 Hatchery opening means Zerg is playing economically but not greedily, and Protoss can follow their standard build without adjustment.

## FFE Execution

Proper cannon placement is the difference between a successful FFE and a lost game. The Forge and cannons must completely wall the natural choke so that zerglings cannot run past. A single gap means lings flood into the natural and kill the building Nexus. One to two cannons at the choke is standard -- more than that wastes minerals that should go into the Nexus and tech. After the Nexus completes, the Gateway goes down, followed by the tech choice: Stargate for corsairs or Citadel of Adun for zealot speed. Stargate is the standard choice because corsairs provide air control that is essential for the mid game.

## Key Decisions

The first major decision after FFE is Stargate versus Citadel. Stargate corsairs kill overlords to deny Zerg supply and vision, control mutalisks, and enable Disruption Web for later pushes. Citadel gives zealot speed for ground-based aggression and faster storm tech. In modern PvZ, Stargate is heavily favored because corsair air control is too important to skip. The second decision is cannon count -- too few cannons and early pressure kills the Nexus, too many and the economy suffers from over-investment in static defense. One to two cannons at the natural is the standard.

## Common Mistakes

Bad cannon placement that leaves a gap for zerglings is the most common early PvZ error. Over-cannoning -- building four or five cannons when two would suffice -- wastes minerals and delays tech. Not scouting mutalisk timing leaves Protoss without corsairs when mutalisks arrive, allowing Zerg to harass mineral lines freely and control the entire map with air units.
