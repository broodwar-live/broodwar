Late-game PvZ is defined by one unit: the Defiler. Once Zerg reaches Hive tech and Defilers enter the battlefield with Dark Swarm, the rules of engagement change entirely. Protoss's ranged units -- dragoons, corsairs, reavers -- cannot fire through the swarm, forcing Protoss to rely on Psionic Storm and splash damage to fight effectively. The late game becomes a war of attrition where High Templar management, expansion control, and army composition determine the outcome.

## Dealing with Defilers

Dark Swarm is a cloud that blocks all ranged physical attacks underneath it. Dragoons, the staple ranged unit of Protoss armies, become completely useless under the swarm. Hydralisks and zerglings, however, can close the distance and fight in melee range where Protoss's ranged advantage evaporates. A well-placed Dark Swarm on an advancing zergling/hydralisk army turns what should be a favorable engagement for Protoss into a devastating loss.

Psionic Storm is the direct counter. Storm deals damage regardless of Dark Swarm -- the damage is spell-based, not a physical attack, so it ignores the swarm entirely. The correct response to Dark Swarm is to storm the units underneath it, then engage with melee units like zealots and Archons who are not affected by the ranged attack restriction. Archons are particularly valuable because their splash damage is classified as a spell attack, allowing them to deal full damage to units under the swarm.

Plague, the Defiler's other key ability, reduces every affected unit's health to a critical level without killing them. Plagued zealots and Archons become fragile and die to minimal follow-up damage. The counter is spreading the army to minimize plague's area of effect and engaging quickly before the health drain becomes lethal in combination with enemy attacks.

## High Templar Management

Energy management across multiple High Templar is the most skill-intensive aspect of late-game PvZ. Each Templar has enough energy for two storms, and storm has a cooldown, so staggering casts across multiple Templar provides continuous storm coverage during extended engagements. Running out of storm energy mid-fight is often game-ending -- without storm, Protoss has no answer to Dark Swarm, and the army collapses.

Depleted High Templar should be merged into Archons immediately. Archons are powerful frontline units with splash damage and high shields, and merging spent Templar converts dead weight into combat power. Fresh High Templar should be continuously produced from Gateways and joined to the army. A Protoss player who maintains a steady cycle of storm casts, Archon merges, and new Templar production has a significant advantage.

Storm drops into Zerg mineral lines remain effective into the late game. Even as the ground war becomes about Dark Swarm and storm exchanges, economic harassment keeps Zerg's drone count under pressure and prevents them from simply outmacro-ing Protoss.

## Army Composition

The standard late-game PvZ army is zealots, Archons, and High Templar. Zealots with speed upgrade serve as the frontline, absorbing damage and engaging zerglings in melee where they trade efficiently. Archons provide splash damage that cuts through zergling and hydralisk swarms, and their high shields make them durable against non-spell damage. High Templar cast storms to break up Zerg formations and counter Dark Swarm.

Corsairs maintain air control throughout the late game. Even after their overlord-hunting role diminishes, they prevent mutalisk harassment, enable storm drops by escorting shuttles, and use Disruption Web on Spore Colonies to create openings. Reavers can be mixed into the composition for additional splash damage, though they are less critical than in the mid game.

## Expansion Wars

Protoss must keep expanding in the late game. The zealot/Archon/storm army is expensive to maintain and replace, and a single lost engagement burns through resources rapidly. Every base should be defended with Photon Cannons to guard against zergling run-bys -- small groups of lings sent to undefended expansions can destroy a Nexus and probes before the army can respond. Zerg will attempt to outexpand Protoss by taking every corner of the map. Zealot run-bys to Zerg expansions, storm drops on drone lines, and corsair overlord hunting all serve to slow Zerg's economic advantage.

## Carrier Transition

Mass Carriers become viable in extreme late-game scenarios when the ground war is stalemated. Interceptors deal consistent damage against ground-only Zerg compositions, and Carriers fly above Dark Swarm. Zerg counters Carriers with Scourge and mass Hydralisks, which can be expensive to sustain. The Carrier transition is a gamble -- committing to air production while maintaining the ground army stretches Protoss's economy, but if Zerg lacks adequate anti-air, a Carrier fleet can end the game.

## Common Mistakes

Running out of High Templar during an engagement is the most common and most punishing error. Without storm, the army has no counter to Dark Swarm and disintegrates. Not expanding aggressively enough allows Zerg to establish an unassailable economic lead through sheer base count. Engaging under Dark Swarm with dragoons instead of retreating and storming wastes the army for nothing -- dragoons literally cannot deal damage under the swarm, and every second spent there is units dying for free.
