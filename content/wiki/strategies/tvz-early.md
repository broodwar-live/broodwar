The first three minutes of TvZ define the entire game. Terran's early game revolves around one requirement: surviving long enough to establish a natural expansion. Every decision -- building placement, scout timing, marine count -- determines whether Terran reaches the mid game with a functional economy or dies to zergling pressure.

## Opening Builds

**Wall-off** is the absolute first priority. A Supply Depot and Barracks at the natural ramp block zergling run-bys. If there is a gap -- even one tile -- zerglings flood in and kill SCVs before enough marines are out. Every competitive map has specific building placements for a tight wall, and learning these is a prerequisite for the matchup.

**1 Rax FE** is the standard safe opening. Barracks as part of the wall, marine production begins, then Command Center at the natural. This sacrifices aggression for economic stability. Marines stay just numerous enough to defend zergling pokes while the second base's income kicks in. From here, Terran transitions into bio or mech depending on scouting information.

**2 Rax Pressure** is more aggressive. Two Barracks produce marines before expanding. The goal is six to eight marines crossing the map to deal drone damage, forcing sunkens or pulling drones to fight. If it works, Terran expands with a lead. If the push accomplishes nothing, Zerg is ahead because Terran delayed their Command Center.

**Bunker Rush** is the highest-variance opening. An SCV crosses the map early and builds a Bunker at the Zerg natural. If completed and filled with marines, the game can end -- Zerg cannot break a repaired Bunker with zerglings alone. If scouted and denied, Terran loses the SCV, minerals, and mining time. Rare in professional play but devastating when it lands.

## Scouting

SCV scout leaves at approximately 9 supply to read the Zerg opening. The critical information is pool timing: Hatchery first (economic) or Spawning Pool first (aggressive)? Count zerglings -- more than six early signals committed aggression. Check for early gas, which means Zerg is teching toward mutalisks or lurkers and changes Terran's mid-game preparation entirely.

## Defense Priorities

Wall-off completes before anything else. Behind it, one to two Bunkers at the natural with SCVs ready to repair handle most early aggression. Against early pool (4-pool or 6-pool), pull SCVs to fight alongside the first marine while the Bunker finishes -- once it is up and repaired, Terran survives. Against hatchery-first Zerg, there is no immediate threat, so Terran expands safely and techs toward an Academy for Stim Pack.

## Transition Points

After securing the natural, the critical decision arrives: bio or mech? Bio means Academy, Stim Pack, Engineering Bay, +1 attack, and a marine/medic timing push at roughly seven minutes. Mech means Factory, Machine Shop, Siege Tech, and vultures with spider mines for map control. The choice depends on the map (open favors mech, choke-heavy favors bio), opponent tendencies, and personal preference. Both paths are viable at every level.

## Common Mistakes

Leaving a gap in the wall-off lets zerglings into the main and usually ends the game. Over-committing to a bunker rush after it is scouted throws away resources. Failing to SCV scout means dying to 4-pool or 6-pool without preparation. Building too many marines before expanding keeps Terran safe but falls behind economically against three-hatchery Zerg.
