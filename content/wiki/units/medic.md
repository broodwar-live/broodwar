The indispensable support unit for Terran bio play. Medics have no attack but heal nearby biological units automatically, dramatically extending the lifespan of Marine-based armies. Introduced in the Brood War expansion, Medics transformed Terran infantry from disposable fodder into a sustained fighting force that can trade efficiently across long engagements.

## Competitive Usage

Medics are mandatory in any bio composition. In TvZ, a typical Marine/Medic army runs a ratio of roughly 3-4 Marines per Medic. This combination allows bio armies to survive Lurker spines and Zergling engagements that would otherwise shred pure Marine groups. In TvP, Medics keep Marines alive through Zealot engagements and enable sustained pushes against gateway armies.

## Key Mechanics

Medics heal 200 HP total before running out of energy (base 50 energy, 200 max). The heal rate is fast enough to keep individual Marines alive through moderate damage. Restoration removes negative effects like Plague, Ensnare, Irradiate, Lockdown, and Parasite — a crucial counter in specific matchups. Optical Flare permanently reduces a unit's sight range to 1, most notably used against Overlords to cripple Zerg detection.

## Micro Techniques

- **Medic positioning**: Keeping Medics behind the Marine line so they heal without being targeted first
- **Optical Flare sniping**: Blinding Overlords before engaging Lurkers so Zerg loses detection coverage
- **Restoration timing**: Saving energy for clutch Restoration on Irradiated units or Plagued groups

## Counters

Medics are a priority target for opponents — killing Medics guts a bio army's sustain. Psionic Storm hits Medics and Marines alike, and smart Protoss players target storms on the Medic cluster. Lurker spines damage both Marines and Medics simultaneously. Since Medics cannot attack, excess Medics reduce army DPS.

## Historical Notes

Before Brood War added Medics, Terran infantry was considered the weakest composition in the game. The Medic's introduction single-handedly created the bio playstyle that has defined competitive TvZ and TvP for over two decades. The Marine/Medic combination remains one of the most elegant design additions in RTS history.
