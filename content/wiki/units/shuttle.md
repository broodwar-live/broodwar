The Protoss transport, carrying up to 8 supply of ground units and enabling some of the most exciting micro-intensive strategies in Brood War. Shuttles are essential for Reaver drops, storm drops, and Zealot bombs — delivering Protoss units where they are needed most. With the speed upgrade from the Robotics Support Bay, Shuttles become fast enough to outrun most anti-air threats, making drop play a viable sustained strategy.

## Versus Zerg

The speed upgrade lets Shuttles deliver Reavers or Zealots into Zerg bases faster than Mutalisks can intercept in PvZ. Shuttle-Reaver drops are the foundation of the Bisu Build that revolutionized the matchup. Storm drops with High Templar devastate Drone lines at Zerg expansions.

## Versus Terran

Shuttle-Reaver drops harass Terran mineral lines and bypass Siege Tank defenses in PvT. Storm drops — loading High Templar into Shuttles to storm workers — are equally devastating. Shuttles also enable Zealot bombs behind tank lines, exploiting the Siege Tank's minimum range dead zone.

## Versus Protoss

Reaver drops define PvP, and the player with better Shuttle micro often wins. The Shuttle-Reaver dance — unloading to fire a Scarab then immediately reloading and repositioning — is the signature micro technique of the mirror matchup. Shuttle speed is critical for executing drops safely and retreating before the opponent can respond.

## Key Mechanics

Shuttles have 60 HP and 60 shields with 1 base armor, making them fragile for a Large unit. The speed upgrade is critical — without it, Shuttles are too slow to execute drop plays safely. Cargo capacity is 8 supply, fitting combinations like 1 Reaver (4) + 2 Zealots (4), or 4 Zealots, or 2 High Templar (4) + 2 Zealots. Loading and unloading has per-unit delays that factor into micro execution.

## Micro Techniques

- **Shuttle-Reaver dance**: The signature Protoss micro — unloading the Reaver to fire a Scarab, then immediately reloading and repositioning
- **Storm drops**: Flying a loaded High Templar over enemy workers, unloading, storming, and reloading
- **Zealot bombs**: Dropping speed Zealots into mineral lines for quick worker kills
- **Retreat micro**: Loading units into the Shuttle when enemy reinforcements arrive, preserving them for future drops

## Counters

Killing the Shuttle destroys the strategy — all units inside die if the Shuttle is destroyed while loaded. Scourge pairs instantly kill Shuttles. Marines, Hydralisks, and Corsairs can shoot Shuttles down. Turrets and Spore Colonies at bases deter drops. Positioning anti-air along common drop paths is the best defense.

## Combat Data

- **Capacity**: 8 cargo slots (Zealot=2, Reaver=4, High Templar=2)
- Speed: 4.43 base → 6.65 with Gravitic Drive upgrade
- 60 HP + 60 shields = 120 total — fragile for a Large unit
- **AI Note**: Shuttles have no auto-micro; every load/unload must be manually controlled
- Shuttle-Reaver micro = most APM-intensive Protoss technique; speed upgrade is mandatory

## Historical Notes

The Shuttle is one of the most skill-defining units in Brood War. The difference between a mediocre and a great Protoss player often comes down to Shuttle micro — the speed of loading, unloading, and repositioning. The Shuttle-Reaver combination is one of the game's signature mechanics and has been a staple of Protoss highlight reels for over two decades.
