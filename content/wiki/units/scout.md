A heavy air fighter with impressive anti-air damage on paper but a troubled competitive legacy. The Scout deals 28 Explosive damage against air targets and 8 Normal damage against ground, with respectable HP (150) and shields (100). However, at 275/125 — one of the most expensive non-capital ships in the game — the Scout is dramatically outclassed by cheaper alternatives like the Corsair for anti-air and the Carrier for heavy air power.

## Versus Zerg

Scouts are virtually never used in PvZ. Corsairs provide superior anti-air at a fraction of the cost, and Mutalisks are Small-sized, reducing the Scout's Explosive damage to 50%. The investment is entirely unjustifiable when cheaper and more effective alternatives exist.

## Versus Terran

Scouts see no practical use in PvT. Dragoons and Corsairs handle all anti-air needs more cost-efficiently. The Scout's weak ground attack provides no meaningful contribution against Terran mech or bio compositions.

## Versus Protoss

Scouts occasionally appear in PvP air builds, where their high single-target air damage can be relevant against enemy Carriers or Shuttles. However, even in this niche scenario, they are generally outclassed by Corsairs and considered one of the least cost-efficient units in the game.

## Key Mechanics

The air attack deals 28 Explosive damage, meaning full damage against Large air units but 75% against Medium and 50% against Small — ironically reducing effectiveness against the units Protoss most needs to counter (Mutalisks are Small, Wraiths are Large). The ground attack is a weak 8 Normal damage, making Scouts nearly useless against ground armies. Scouts are fast (6.67 speed) and Large-sized.

## Micro Techniques

- **Focus fire**: Targeting individual expensive air units where the Scout's high single-target damage matters most
- **Hit-and-run**: Using the Scout's high speed to engage and disengage against slower air targets
- **Air patrol**: Using Scouts to establish air superiority over specific map areas

## Counters

Almost everything counters the Scout from a cost-efficiency perspective. Corsairs with splash damage outperform Scouts against clumped air units for less cost. Hydralisks and Marines provide cheaper anti-air from the ground. Devourers with acid spore stacking can neutralize Scouts. Goliaths with Charon Boosters outrange and outdamage them.

## Historical Notes

The Scout is widely considered the most poorly balanced unit in Brood War — not because it is weak in absolute terms, but because its cost makes it strictly inferior to alternatives in every realistic scenario. Despite periodic discussions in the community about Scout buffs, Blizzard never adjusted its cost, leaving it as a fascinating design artifact. The Scout serves as a reminder that raw stats alone do not determine a unit's competitive viability.
