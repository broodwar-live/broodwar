The quintessential swarm unit — two Zerglings hatch from a single larva for just 50 minerals, making them the most cost-efficient combat unit in the game by volume. Zerglings are lightning-fast melee attackers that excel at surrounding enemies, running past defenses into unprotected bases, and overwhelming opponents with sheer numbers. With the Metabolic Boost speed upgrade, they become the fastest ground unit alongside Vultures.

## Competitive Usage

Zerglings are critical in every Zerg matchup. In ZvT, speed Zerglings provide early defense against Vulture harassment, perform run-bys into Terran bases, and in the late game with Adrenal Glands, they become terrifying damage dealers under Dark Swarm. In ZvP, Zergling surrounds on Dragoons and Zealots are a defining skill, and Zergling run-bys into Protoss bases force constant defensive attention. In ZvZ, early Zergling pressure often decides the game before tech units arrive.

## Key Mechanics

Zerglings deal 5 Normal damage per hit with an extremely fast attack cooldown of 8 frames (the fastest in the game). Metabolic Boost from the Spawning Pool dramatically increases movement speed, making it the single most important early-game upgrade for Zerg. Adrenal Glands from the Hive nearly doubles attack speed, transforming late-game Zerglings into devastating damage dealers. Zerglings are Small-sized, taking reduced damage from Explosive attacks.

## Micro Techniques

- **Surrounds**: Encircling enemy units with Zerglings to maximize the number attacking simultaneously and prevent escape
- **Run-bys**: Sending Zerglings past the enemy army into their base to attack workers and buildings
- **Burrow ambush**: Burrowing Zerglings along paths and unburrowing to ambush passing armies or workers
- **Split micro**: Splitting Zerglings into multiple groups to attack different locations simultaneously

## Counters

Zerglings are hard-countered by Firebats (Concussive splash), Siege Tanks (area splash), and Lurkers (line splash). Wall-offs at ramps prevent early Zergling rushes. High Templar storms devastate clumped Zerglings. Any air unit is immune to Zerglings. Vulture Spider Mines can destroy entire Zergling groups.

## Historical Notes

The "6-pool" (earliest possible Zergling rush) has been a feared cheese strategy since StarCraft's launch. In the modern era, Jaedong's masterful Zergling control — surrounding entire armies, executing perfect run-bys, and maintaining map presence — set the standard for Zerg play. The Zergling remains the most-produced unit in competitive Brood War across all three races.
