The Zerg worker unit, uniquely among all three races, is consumed when it morphs into a building. Every Zerg structure costs not only minerals and gas but also a Drone — making each building decision a direct trade between economy and infrastructure. This mechanic defines Zerg's fundamental strategic tension: building too many structures guts the economy, while building too few leaves the Zerg technologically behind.

## Competitive Usage

Drone management is the core skill of Zerg play. The decision of when to build Drones versus when to build units — called "droning up" — is the single most important macro decision in competitive Zerg. Producing Drones from larvae competes directly with producing combat units, creating a constant tension. The optimal Drone count varies by matchup and timing: too few means falling behind economically, too many means dying to an attack.

## Key Mechanics

Drones deal 5 Normal damage in melee and have 40 HP, making them the second-weakest worker in combat (Probes are weaker). They harvest 8 minerals per trip. When morphing into a building, the Drone is consumed — if the building is canceled, the Drone is lost. This makes Zerg building placement mistakes costlier than for other races. Drones are Small-sized and can burrow with the Burrow upgrade. They are produced from larvae at the Hatchery.

## Micro Techniques

- **Drone drilling**: Using the mineral walk command (Return Cargo) to move a Drone through units, allowing it to pass through buildings or enemy units
- **Drone scout**: Sending an early Drone to the enemy base to identify their build order and tech path
- **Drone pull defense**: Pulling Drones from the mineral line to fight off early aggression when no military units are available
- **Extractor trick**: Morphing a Drone into an Extractor when supply-blocked to free up a larva slot, then canceling the Extractor

## Counters

Any combat unit kills Drones quickly. Worker harassment from Vultures, Zealots, or Marines targets the Zerg economy directly. Reaver drops into the mineral line are devastating. Since each Drone lost also impacts future building options, Drone losses cascade — losing workers means slower recovery than for other races.

## Historical Notes

The Drone's sacrifice mechanic is one of the most distinctive design choices in StarCraft. It creates Zerg's unique macro rhythm — the "drone or army" decision — that has no parallel in the other races. Professional Zerg players spend as much mental energy on Drone timing as on unit micro. The concept of "droning behind an attack" — building economy while launching aggression — is a hallmark of advanced Zerg play that has been refined over two decades of competitive evolution.
