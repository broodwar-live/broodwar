The Protoss worker unit, a small robotic drone that harvests resources and warps in buildings through dimensional recall. Unlike SCVs and Drones, Probes do not stay at the construction site — once the warp-in is initiated, the Probe is free to return to mining while the building constructs itself. This unique mechanic gives Protoss an economic edge during building placement but means buildings cannot be repaired during construction.

## Competitive Usage

Probes play surprising tactical roles beyond their economic function. The cannon rush (Forge-first into Photon Cannons at the enemy base) is one of the most iconic cheese strategies, using a Probe to warp in Pylons and Cannons in the enemy's base or natural. Mineral walk micro — using the return cargo command to move through blocking units — makes Probes effective scouts in the early game, as they can slip past enemy workers and buildings.

## Key Mechanics

Probes deal 5 Normal damage in melee, the weakest of all worker units. They have 20 HP and 20 shields, making them the most fragile worker. Warp-in buildings require a Pylon's power field and cost the full mineral/gas price up front, but the Probe returns to mining immediately. If a warp-in is canceled, Protoss gets a partial resource refund. Each Probe harvests 8 minerals per trip, identical to other workers.

## Micro Techniques

- **Cannon rush**: Warping in Pylons and Photon Cannons at the enemy base or natural, using the Probe's freedom to place multiple buildings rapidly
- **Mineral walk scouting**: Using Return Cargo to walk through enemy units or wall-offs to scout inside the enemy base
- **Probe harassment**: In PvP, early Probe attacks on the enemy Probe line to slow their economy
- **Quick wall-in**: Rapidly placing buildings to wall off ramps or choke points against early rushes

## Counters

Probes are the weakest workers in combat, losing fights to SCVs and Drones one-on-one. Any military unit kills Probes quickly. Worker pulls against Protoss are less effective because Probes have shields that need to be burned through. Probe cannon rushes are countered by pulling workers to destroy the Pylon or Cannon before it finishes warping in.

## Historical Notes

The cannon rush has been a constant in competitive Brood War, from the earliest days through the modern era. The Probe's ability to warp in buildings and immediately return to work creates the unique "cannon rush" dynamic where a single Probe can build an entire offensive forward base. Probe mineral walking is one of the subtler micro techniques in the game, occasionally appearing in professional matches at decisive moments.
