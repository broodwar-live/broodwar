The Bunker is a Terran defensive structure that garrisons up to 4 infantry units, extending their attack range and providing 350 HP of additional protection. Garrisoned Marines gain +1 range, making them effective against early rushes and harassment. Firebats inside Bunkers also benefit from the range extension, dealing devastating damage to clustered melee units. Bunkers can be salvaged for a 75% mineral refund, making them cost-efficient for temporary defense.

In competitive play, Bunkers are critical for early-game defense, especially in TvZ. Placing a Bunker at the natural ramp or choke point is standard against early Zergling pressure. The Bunker rush is a well-known aggressive strategy where a Terran player builds a Bunker near the enemy base and fills it with Marines for early aggression. SCVs can repair Bunkers during engagements, making them extremely durable when actively managed. Multiple Bunkers with repair can hold off significantly larger forces.

Bunker placement is a critical skill in competitive Terran play. Positioning at the natural choke maximizes defensive coverage, while Bunkers at expansions protect against run-bys. In TvZ, the forward Bunker placement determines whether the Terran can hold the natural expansion against early pool builds. Bunkers do not lift off and cannot be repositioned once built, so correct initial placement is essential. The salvage mechanic allows players to recoup minerals once the defensive need has passed.
