The Pylon is the fundamental Protoss support structure, providing 8 supply and generating a psionic matrix power field. Nearly all Protoss buildings (except the Nexus and Assimilator) must be placed within a Pylon's power field to function. If a Pylon is destroyed and nearby buildings lose power coverage, those buildings are immediately disabled — they cannot produce units, research upgrades, or perform any functions until power is restored by another Pylon.

In competitive play, Pylon placement is one of the most important and nuanced aspects of Protoss strategy. Skilled players ensure overlapping Pylon coverage so that losing a single Pylon does not disable critical production buildings. A proxy Pylon placed near the enemy base or at a forward position allows Protoss to warp in reinforcements closer to the front line, reducing travel time significantly. Pylon scouting — sending the first Pylon-building Probe into the enemy base — is a standard early-game technique.

Strategic Pylon placement involves balancing coverage, redundancy, and scouting. Production Pylons should be doubled up to prevent a single Pylon snipe from shutting down multiple Gateways. Wall-off Pylons at the natural expansion help block paths in PvZ. Pylons at key map positions provide vision for map awareness. With 300 HP and 300 shields, Pylons are relatively durable but still vulnerable to focused attacks. Losing a critical Pylon at the wrong moment — such as one powering multiple Gateways during a battle — can be game-ending.
