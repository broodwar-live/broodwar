The Gateway is the primary Protoss military production building, producing Zealots by default, Dragoons once a Cybernetics Core is built, and High Templar and Dark Templar once Templar Archives are completed. It is the first military structure available to Protoss and the most important production building throughout the game. Unlike Terran's Barracks, the Gateway has no add-ons.

In competitive play, the number of Gateways defines Protoss build orders and production capacity. 1-Gate openings are standard for tech-focused builds, while 2-Gate builds provide early military pressure and are common in PvP. 3-Gate and 4-Gate timings are well-known aggressive strategies. As the game progresses, Protoss players add Gateways to match their income — 5 to 8 Gateways are typical in the mid-to-late game for sustained army production. Gateway count is one of the key scouting indicators opponents look for.

Gateways must be placed within a Pylon's power field. Losing the Pylon powering Gateways disables production, so overlapping Pylon coverage is critical. Proxy Gateways — built near the enemy base — enable aggressive Zealot rushes that can be devastating if unscouted. Forward Gateways near the front lines reduce reinforcement travel time. In the main base, Gateways are often clustered near rally points for efficient unit movement.
