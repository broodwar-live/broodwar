The Extractor is the Zerg gas harvesting structure, built on a Vespene Geyser to enable gas collection. Like all Zerg buildings, constructing an Extractor consumes the Drone that builds it, meaning the Zerg player temporarily loses a worker and 50 minerals in the process. Three Drones assigned to an Extractor is the optimal number for maximum gas income.

In competitive play, the timing of the first Extractor is an important strategic decision. Early gas enables faster tech (Lair, Spire, or Lurker Aspect), while delaying gas allows for more Drone production and a stronger economy. The "Extractor trick" is a well-known technique where a player builds an Extractor to free up a supply slot (since the Drone morphs into the building), hatches a unit from a larva, and then cancels the Extractor to get the minerals back — effectively producing one unit beyond the current supply cap.

The Extractor must be placed on a Vespene Geyser, so its position is fixed. Unlike Terran's Refinery or Protoss's Assimilator, the Extractor consumes the building Drone rather than simply occupying it during construction. This means building an Extractor costs the Zerg player both the 50 mineral building cost and the replacement Drone cost. Must be built on creep only if the geyser is on creep, though geysers at expansion locations can be built on without creep.
