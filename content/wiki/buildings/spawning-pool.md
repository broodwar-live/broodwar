The Spawning Pool is the first and most fundamental Zerg tech building, enabling Zergling production from the Hatchery and serving as the prerequisite for all other Zerg tech structures. It researches Metabolic Boost (Zergling speed upgrade, commonly called "speed" or "ling speed") and Adrenal Glands (Zergling attack speed, requiring a Hive). The Spawning Pool is also required before Sunken Colonies can be morphed from Creep Colonies, and before a Hatchery can morph into a Lair.

In competitive play, the timing of the Spawning Pool defines Zerg's early game more than any other decision. A 4-Pool or 5-Pool is an extreme rush, sacrificing economy for the earliest possible Zerglings. A 9-Pool is an aggressive standard opening. A 12-Pool (or Overpool — Overlord then Pool at 9 supply) balances early defense with economy. A 12-Hatch (Hatchery before Pool) prioritizes economy but is vulnerable to early aggression. These pool timings are the most scouted and analyzed aspect of Zerg openers in professional play.

The Spawning Pool must be built on creep. It is typically placed at the main base near the Hatchery. Since it consumes a Drone to build (like all Zerg structures), the Pool timing also affects Drone count and economy. Early Metabolic Boost research is critical in most matchups — speed Zerglings are dramatically more effective for both offense and defense. Losing the Spawning Pool before it completes in an early-game scenario can be game-ending, as it delays all subsequent Zerg tech.
