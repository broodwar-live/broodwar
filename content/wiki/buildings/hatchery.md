The Hatchery is the primary Zerg base structure, serving simultaneously as the main base, production facility, and supply provider. It passively generates larvae (up to 3 at a time), which morph into all Zerg ground and air units including Drones, Zerglings, Hydralisks, Mutalisks, and more. Each Hatchery provides 1 supply (2 as Lair or Hive). The Hatchery morphs into a Lair (requiring a Spawning Pool) and then into a Hive (requiring a Queen's Nest), unlocking progressively higher tiers of Zerg technology.

In competitive play, the Hatchery is unique among main base structures because additional Hatcheries serve as both expansions and production multipliers. "3 Hatch" builds (building a third Hatchery before or alongside the Spawning Pool) are foundational Zerg economy openings that prioritize larva production and Drone count. More Hatcheries means more larvae, which means more units or workers can be produced simultaneously. This dual economic-production role is central to Zerg strategy — Zerg players constantly balance between building additional Hatcheries for production and spending on army or tech.

The Hatchery does not require creep to be built at expansion locations — it generates its own creep upon completion. Placement at natural expansions is standard, positioned to optimize mineral and gas collection. Macro Hatcheries (additional Hatcheries at the main base solely for extra larvae) are common in late-game Zerg play. Losing a Hatchery is devastating both economically and for production capacity, making base defense a constant priority for Zerg players.
