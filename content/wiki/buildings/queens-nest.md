The Queen's Nest is a Zerg tech building that enables Queen production from the Hatchery and, more importantly, is required to morph a Lair into a Hive. It researches Queen abilities: Spawn Broodling (instantly kills a non-robotic ground unit and spawns two Broodlings), Ensnare (slows enemy movement and attack speed in an area, also reveals cloaked units), and Parasite (places a permanent tracker on an enemy unit, sharing its vision).

In competitive play, the Queen's Nest is built primarily as a prerequisite for the Hive, not for Queen production itself. Queens are rarely produced in professional games, though Spawn Broodling and Ensnare have niche uses. The Hive unlocks critical late-game tech: Adrenal Glands (Zergling attack speed), Greater Spire (Guardians and Devourers), Defiler Mound (Defilers with Dark Swarm and Plague), and Ultralisk Cavern. Because the Queen's Nest gates access to all Hive-tier technology, its timing is a key indicator of when a Zerg player plans to transition to late-game compositions.

The Queen's Nest must be built on creep and requires a Lair. It is typically placed at the main base without special positioning requirements. Since it is almost exclusively a Hive prerequisite in competitive play, it is built as quickly as the gas economy allows when transitioning to Hive tech. Losing a Queen's Nest delays the Hive morphing and consequently all Hive-tier technology, which can be strategically devastating if the Zerg player is counting on late-game Defiler or Ultralisk compositions.
